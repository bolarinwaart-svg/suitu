# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Online shoppers looking for tailored suits, alterations, and occasion-ready formalwear. They should quickly understand the offer, trust the store's taste level, and move from visual discovery to popular product categories without friction.

## Product Purpose

Suit U is a modern ecommerce storefront for curated suit products and tailoring services. The site exists to present strong product imagery, make best-seller and category discovery easy to scan, and create enough confidence for shoppers to explore styles, preorders, and wardrobe upgrades.

Success looks like a polished, trustworthy homepage with stable image delivery, clear product cards, responsive layouts, and a visual system that feels specific to tailoring rather than generic ecommerce.

## Brand Personality

Clean, premium, direct, and visually confident. The voice should feel composed and assured: focused on quality tailoring, strong silhouettes, and useful product discovery rather than vague lifestyle claims.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic ecommerce layouts, vague promotional copy, excessive animations, noisy UI, template-like product grids, and visuals that make tailoring feel ordinary. Do not bury the product photography under decorative effects or use motion that competes with shopping.

## Design Principles

- Product imagery leads: suits, fabric, fit, and styling should do the primary persuasive work.
- Confidence over decoration: every section should feel deliberate, with strong hierarchy and restrained interface noise.
- Discovery stays immediate: categories, best sellers, and CTAs should be easy to scan without weakening the premium tone.
- Performance is part of trust: image priority, lazy loading, and layout stability should protect the first impression.
- Tailoring should feel specific: copy and composition should point to fit, alteration, occasion, and wardrobe upgrade needs.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use WCAG AA as the baseline. Preserve readable contrast, keyboard-accessible controls, meaningful alt text for product imagery, empty alt text only for decorative images, and reduced-motion support for any animation. Layouts should remain usable across mobile and desktop viewports without text overflow.
